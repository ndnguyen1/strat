Executive Summary
Long-Dated Convertible Debt at Cost of Capital

EthStrategy's permissionless bonding allows bonders to attain long-dated convertible debt for essentially cost of capital. Maximum loss is bounded by opportunity cost and smart contract risk — not principal. The debt redemption mechanism structurally eliminates downside beyond these factors.

Product Overview
Two-Component Convertible Instrument
Component 1 · Fungible
CDT
Debt token equivalent to the nominal amount bonded. Transferable and tradeable on secondary markets before exercise.
Component 2 · Non-Fungible
4Y Call Option
American-style, convertible to ETH or STRAT. Strike set OTM at issuance via k × D/E. Transferable.

Any time between bonding and expiry the call option can be exercised. At maturity, both the CDT and option must be returned together to redeem full par value.

Payoff Profile
Two Scenarios
Scenario 1 · Bull — Any Time Before Expiry
ETH rises above the strike price
The call option can be exercised at any point during the 4-year window — not just at maturity. Once ETH crosses the strike, option holders profit immediately. STRAT sells ETH at strike; the OTM buffer at issuance means STRAT still captures extra upside they wouldn't have had.
Scenario 2 · Bear — Only After 4 Years
ETH remains below the strike at maturity
The option expires worthless, but only after the full 4-year term can bonders redeem. They return both CDT and option to STRAT and receive back the full par value of the debt. Principal is fully recovered — maximum loss is opportunity cost only.

CDT & Liquidity
Fungible + Non-Fungible Architecture
Interim Liquidity via CDT

Convertible debt consists of two components: one fungible (CDT) and one non-fungible (option). Before option exercise, CDT can be sold, providing bonders with interim liquidity. CDT will then need to be bought back from the market to exercise or redeem at maturity.

Accessing interim liquidity also opens the possibility for looping strategies — sell CDT, re-bond the proceeds, and compound ETH-linked exposure.

Strike Price Mechanism
Dynamic OTM Strike

The strike is set at issuance based on the current debt-to-equity ratio of the treasury. Higher leverage → deeper OTM buffer for STRAT.

% OTM = k × D/E
k = protocol constant
D = total CDT outstanding · E = treasury GAV

At the current debt load of ~14.2% D/GAV, strikes are set modestly OTM — keeping options attractive to bonders while providing STRAT with a meaningful upside buffer.

Counterparty
STRAT Treasury

Underwriting these options is STRAT, a treasury of ~12k ETH composed of irrationally bullish ETH bulls. These bulls don't care about the price of ETH next day/week/month — they believe ETH, in 4 years, will be significantly higher.

What STRAT wants is a way to lever up on ETH without liquidation risk. When bonders bond dollars, they lend to STRAT, which instantly buys ETH.

For the extra leverage, they're happy to give away their gamma in the form of the option.
